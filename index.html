Arsenal Football Club

over the years

Intro

Arsenal football club, popularly known as the Gunners, was founded in 1886 by munitions workers at the Royal Arsenal. The Club is renowned for its heritage and history. The following are the eight unique facts about Arsenal that have contributed to the club's popularity amongst fans. Move over the images to know more.

The Gunners

Arsenal are nicknamed ‘The Gunners’, as club was formed by a group of cannon makers at the Woolwich Arsenal in 1886.

Never Relegated

Arsenal are the only team still in existence that has never been relegated. They have been in the top flight of English football since the 1919/20 campaign.

The Invinvibles

A gold version of the Premier League trophy to commemorate the achievement of going unbeaten on their way to winning the title during the 2003/04 season.

Radio Broadcast

Arsenal’s game against Sheffield United on 22nd January 1927 at Highbury was the first ever English league match to be broadcast on the radio.

Live Broadcast

The game between Arsenal’s first team and and reserves was the first ever football match to be televised live, on 16th September 1937.

Cup Double 1993

In 1993, Arsenal became the first club to win the League Cup and FA Cup in the same year.

3D Broadcast

On 31st January 2010, Arsenal’s game against Manchester United was the first match to be broadcast on television in 3D.

Thierry Henry "Titi"

Striker Sensation, Arsenal's Leading all-time goal scorer with 228 in all competitions. 4-time winner of "Premier League Golden Boot".

It is in both supporters and the club's best interests to study Arsenal's on-field and financial excellence to shed light on the club's growth and offer business models that can be implemented by looking at other big 5 clubs to maximise the club's success.
Appraisal on Performance

Arsenal's points tally of the season has steadily declined since 2006, resulting in a consistent finish outside the top 4 from the 2016/17 season. The critical events in the club's history, managers, owners' investments, player acquisitions, player compensation, and the club's market value are investigated to explore the factors for deterioration in sporting performance and finances.

Managers and Contributions

Managers play an essential role in the club since they have a voice in player acquisitions, lead training sessions to coach the players, develop the style of play, choose the team and formation. The football operations staff, which includes the CEO, Technical Director, contract negotiators, and scouting crew, will assist the manager in running the club on a daily basis.

Significant events in the Arsenal football club, such as stadium re-location, trophy haul, operating model of the club, and managers' influence on club status, were analysed to establish the factors that contributed to the team's success and failure. The club's previous 5 managers were studied to assess the impact of managers on the club's stature and on-field performance.

Verdict of Managers

It is clear that David Dein, Arsenal's vice president, took a courageous decision to appoint a foreign manager, Arsene Wenger, in August 1996, succeeding Bruce Rioch. Arsene's contribution to sporting glory is enormous. His first six seasons from 1996 were a dynasty in terms of trophies and league standings, with Arsenal never finishing lower than second in the league. One of the most remarkable seasons in Premier League history was Arsenal's undefeated season in 2003/04, when they finished with 90 points, 26 wins, 12 draws, and 0 defeats. Arsenal was branded the "Invincibles" and bestowed the golden Premier League trophy to celebrate their unbeaten season. George Graham established the groundwork for the squad that Arsene took over by making clever acquisitions. He is rightfully known as "The Architect Of Arsenal's 1980's Revival."

Relocation from Highbury to Emirates

The Arsenal Board of Directors decided to relocate the club from Highbury to Emirates in order for the club to compete among the premier clubs in Europe. The new stadium has a higher seating capacity and hospitality facilities, which intend to produce more revenue. A £260 million commercial loan with a 14-year tenure was arranged to finance stadium development. The loan repayment was contingent on the club's on-field and financial s1uccess. The impact of relocation on modern-day Arsenal is analysed to provide insight into the club's performance.
Revenue Boost from Emirates

On October 8, 2006, Arsneal relocated to Emirates. In comparison to the previous season, the club collected more than 30 million pounds in income on matchday. This enabled Arsenal to compete with Europe's premier teams and to recoup loans. Arsene's remarkable accomplishment is to transition from Higbury to Emirates and participate in the Champions League during the stadium's building and payback time.

Impact of Transfers on Performance

Arsenal's player signings in the last 5 seasons are compared with the transfers of the Premier League's other five significant teams. The transfer rating and resale rating were used to categorise transfers. Transfer rating relates to on-field player stats like assists, goals, successful passes per 90 minutes, interceptions, clearances, clean sheet, and saves based on the player's position. The resale rating is related to the resale value and the player's age. Both are evaluated on a four-point scale.
Player Acquisition Abysmal

Arsenal made 22 player transfers between 2015/16 and 2020/21 seasons. The majority of the transfers did not add investment value to the asset. Most of the players scouted and purchased by the club failed to hit the ground running and consistently perform. On the other hand Manchester City and Liverpool have the most successful transfers, demonstrating the efficacy of football operations.

Impact of wage bill and Net spend on the Performance

Football management relies heavily on negotiations. Contract negotiators from the football operations staff oversee the negotiations with the assistance of the sporting director and manager. The net expenditure on player acquisitions and average salary of players during the previous five seasons are compared to the net spend and average wage of the other five big clus into the league to provide insight on the effectiveness of Arsenal's negotiators.
Appalling Pay Structure

Arsenal pays under-performing players more than its rivals. Over the preceding five seasons, Arsenal's average finish position was lowest among the six major teams. Arsenal spent money on players; however, the players' signed did not adapt to the league and live up to the money spent on player acquisition. Flop signing highlights the poor strategy adopted by the scouting, management, and technical director in identifying the players.

Relation between Market Worth and Performance

The relationship between club performance and football operations was explored with the club's market worth over a decade. The market value of a football club is determined by the club's prospects, the player's age, performance, injury susceptibility, the general evolution of transfer fees, particular transfer modalities, and situational variables. The market worth of Gunners over a decade is compared to the other five clubs to demonstrate the success of the football operations team.
Minimal Market Growth

The market worth of all six teams has increased over the previous decade. However, Arsenal's market value increase has been disproportionate to the other five major clubs, with the smallest growth among other clubs. All teams had a negative trend in 2019 and 2020 due to COVID-19. Football games were held behind closed doors with no fan representation, resulting in a loss of match day revenue.

To summarise Arsenal football club over the years, the team's on-field performances have continuously declined in the previous five seasons. The Gunners have been looking for a place in the Champions League since the retirement of Arsene Wenger in 2016. He is the most notable manager in the club's history for his on-field achievements with trophies and contributions to the club's financial stability. Relocation from Highbury to Emirates has boosted Arsenal's revenue, allowing the club to operate on a self-sustaining model without the investment from owner. Arsenal's football operations have been ineffective in player signings and financial negotiations. The club hired non-performing players on higher wages, resulting in a pay per performance disparity compared with the league's top teams. Arsenal's market worth has not increased compared to other teams in the league due to mismanagement by football operations. Arsenal can take inspiration from Liverpool's operating model to effectively engage in transfer and contract negotiations to recruit the right player at the best price and pay. Football operates best when multiple football operations departments collaborate, which is now lacking at Arsenal. To return to past glory, Arsenal requires a reshuffle and a breath of fresh air in the operations staff and financial backing from the owners to buy the appropriate players for the squad.
